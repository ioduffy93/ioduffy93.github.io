Stage 1: Consumer Profiling

To Better Understand Iceland's Consumers and Improve Targeting

Why?

To get in-depth understanding of the needs, experiences and motivations to shop amongst current, lapsed and non-Iceland shoppers.

The insights gained at this stage will then also feed into the content for our quantitative testing, as we’ll get an understanding of the consumer language used when they are talking about Iceland and what the consumers themselves believe differentiates other grocery stores in the market.

Who?

I will ask 10 UK wide participants who are either the main or share the household food shop, over the course of 5 days, to write a personal WordPress blog.

10x Online Blogs running for 5 days

4 x Currently shop at Iceland

4 x Aware of (& potentially shop at) Iceland

2 x Unaware

5x Male and Female

Spread equally across all age brackets (from 18+)

What?

Over 5 days, each participant will have their own private blogs to answer the following question areas I put to them:

  • Who they are

  • Attitudes & key drivers

  • Grocery shop preference & choices

  • Perceptions of Iceland

    • Why Blogs?

    Online blogs are a time and cost-effective technique to really delve into the consumer mindset – a natural environment for our target audience to story-tell and freely express & share their opinions and thoughts. This format allows us and participants to share images, and for us to adapt our questions and tasks each day depending on the answers and insights acquired.
